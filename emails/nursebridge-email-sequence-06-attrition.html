Nursing Student Retention & Readiness

1 in 5

nursing students do not graduate

   

Nationally, nursing programs lose about one in five students before graduation — and some programs report attrition rates as high as 50%.

What the data also reflects

20% → 26.5%

Dropout Rate Increase

Over one decade in one Dutch nursing program — while five-year graduation rates dropped from 56.9% to 39.8%.

Qualified Applications Not Accepted

Reported by AACN in one cycle — reinforcing the pressure surrounding nursing education, workforce shortages, and student retention.

What the data does not show

Many students do not leave because they lack intelligence, motivation, or the ability to become excellent nurses.

   

They leave because the pressure becomes overwhelming.

Pressures that quietly compound

Academic rigor & clinical expectations   Emotional exhaustion
Financial strain   Caregiving responsibilities
Transportation barriers   Burnout progression
Stress escalation   Lack of stabilization support

These pressures can quietly compound until students begin disengaging academically, emotionally, or both.
